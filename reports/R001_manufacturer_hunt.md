# Atlas Sprint 001 — Turkey Manufacturer Target List

## 1. Executive Summary

- **The first actionable longlist contains 50 verified manufacturer candidates:** 12 from Italy, 10 each from Poland, Taiwan and South Korea, and 8 from the Czech Republic. The mix is 24 valve manufacturers, 15 sealing manufacturers and 11 filtration manufacturers.
- **Forty-two candidates meet the desk-research `GO` threshold, but this is a prospecting gate rather than completed due diligence.** Seven are `WATCH` and one is `NO-GO`. Negative Turkey-presence findings mean no obvious office or named distributor was found in this public review; they are not proof of channel absence.
- **The best first-outreach cluster is specialized seals and replacement filtration.** These products combine technical qualification with recurring consumption, installed-base replacement demand and a credible commission/representation model.
- **Recommended first wave:** SPETECH, Erving Seal, SEDA Filter, Roten and Kingtron. This group balances very high scores, export evidence, technical differentiation and likely openness to a low-fixed-cost market test.

## 2. What Was Searched

The search was limited to real B2B manufacturers in the requested product families and countries:

- Industrial and process valves: ball, gate, globe, check, butterfly, cryogenic, high-pressure and special-alloy valves.
- Mechanical seals and sealing: pump and mixer seals, cartridge seals, oil seals, O-rings, high-duty gaskets and engineered packing.
- Industrial filtration: liquid, air, gas, hydraulic and process filter elements; filter presses; dust extraction and separation systems.

Manufacturer websites, technical catalogues, certification pages, company histories, trade-agency catalogues and credible industry profiles were used. Distributor-only businesses, marketplaces, commodity hardware sellers and companies without identifiable production evidence were excluded.

## 3. Search Methodology

This is a source-backed commercial screening, not a claim of completed legal or channel due diligence.

1. A candidate entered the master only when at least one public source showed manufacturing evidence such as a factory, production process, in-house engineering, manufacturing certificate scope or explicit manufacturer statement.
2. Export readiness was assessed from foreign markets, export history, multilingual catalogues, international approvals, overseas references or explicit OEM/ODM positioning.
3. Product attractiveness was scored for repeat purchasing, technical barriers, Turkey industry relevance, differentiation, margin potential and apparent openness to representation.
4. Trust and commercial scores were applied using the weights in the Sprint brief. Atlas Score is the arithmetic mean of the two component scores.
5. `GO` was used only where both component scores are at least 75, export potential is visible and no obvious strong Turkey distributor was found. `WATCH` marks unresolved channel maturity, weak export proof or another material gap. `NO-GO` marks a clear size/channel mismatch.
6. Names, certifications and emails were recorded only when visible in a reviewed source. Missing fields are `UNKNOWN`. Employee bands are reasoned public-profile estimates and must be confirmed.

Coverage is intentionally balanced across all five target countries rather than allowing Italy's much larger visible supplier universe to dominate the list.

## 5. Elite 5 Recommended for First Outreach

### 1. SPETECH — Poland

**Why first:** It combines the strongest evidence profile with a focused, high-value product set. The company states 30 years in industrial sealing, Polish production and supply to 100 countries. Its special gaskets and packings fit Turkish refineries, chemical plants, pressure-equipment fabricators and energy accounts.

**Opening angle:** a low-risk Turkey market-development pilot built around named end-user mapping, specification support and project registration, with no fixed local payroll.

### 2. Erving Seal — Taiwan

**Why first:** It explicitly presents itself as both manufacturer and exporter, publishes a direct email, and supports custom mechanical-seal configurations. This is unusually aligned with an independent market-development partner.

**Opening angle:** pump and rotating-equipment aftermarket access across water, chemicals, food processing and industrial maintenance.

### 3. SEDA Filter — South Korea

**Why first:** The company states 35 years of OEM manufacturing, Korean factory production, sales to more than 50 countries and references to major engine and heavy-industry OEMs. Filter elements create repeat orders after initial qualification.

**Opening angle:** Turkey replacement-filter development for marine engines, power generation, mining and heavy diesel fleets, supported by local application discovery.

### 4. Roten — Italy

**Why first:** Roten offers the strongest combination of heritage, certifications, technical depth and broad process-industry relevance among the Italian seal candidates. Mechanical seals also fit a representation model better than one-off capital equipment.

**Opening angle:** account mapping for pump users and repair channels in water, food, chemical and pharmaceutical production.

### 5. Kingtron — Taiwan

**Why first:** Kingtron openly targets OEM/ODM and private-label partners, and its certified high-pressure valve range is suited to skid builders, EPC projects and specialized distributors.

**Opening angle:** distributor-neutral project registration and OEM introductions to Turkish package-equipment builders and process-industry accounts.

Valtec and ANGA are immediate alternates. They scored comparably, but the Elite 5 creates a more useful first-wave mix across three product families and four countries.

## 6. Key Risks

- **Turkey channel invisibility:** distributor agreements are frequently unpublished, local-language only or handled through regional partners. Every Elite target needs a direct conflict check before a pitch.
- **Estimated company size:** employee ranges are screening estimates, not audited headcounts. Some candidates may sit outside the preferred 30–500 range.
- **Certificate currency:** a certificate named in a catalogue may have expired or been superseded. Request the current certificate pack and scope before representing compliance to customers.
- **Manufacturer versus hybrid supplier:** GTM and Kragum visibly combine own production with distributed products. Any agreement should define the own-manufactured SKUs in scope.
- **Project-cycle mismatch:** high-specification valves can take months to qualify and may require approved-vendor-list entry. Seals and filter elements should therefore lead the first outreach wave.
- **Public evidence bias:** strong digital presentation lifts trust scores. A technically excellent but low-visibility manufacturer may be under-ranked, while polished claims still require direct validation.

## 7. Rejected Patterns

The research rejected or avoided:

- Valve and filter distributors presenting third-party catalogues as a broad “product programme.”
- Companies whose only evidence was a marketplace listing, generic directory page or unverifiable export portal.
- Commodity plumbing and residential water-filter sellers without industrial process applications.
- Firms offering only stock resale, warehouse availability or “brands represented,” with no defensible production evidence.
- Very large global groups where an independent Turkey representation mandate is improbable; NAK remains in the master as a scored `NO-GO` benchmark.
- Companies with thin websites, no physical identity, copied claims, missing technical material or certification assertions that could not be tied to the company.
- Candidates with obvious channel maturity or possible established distribution that could not be cleared; these were placed on `WATCH`, not promoted to first contact.

## 8. Next Action Recommendation

Run a two-step qualification sprint before drafting personalized outreach:

1. **Channel-conflict verification:** search Turkish-language sources, trade-fair exhibitor lists, import records where lawful, LinkedIn employees and manufacturer contact forms for the Elite 5 plus alternates Valtec and ANGA. Record any named importer, distributor or Turkish project office.
2. **Contact enrichment:** identify the current Export Manager, International Sales Manager or Managing Director from first-party sources or confirmed professional profiles. Do not infer email patterns.
3. **Prepare seven one-page account hypotheses:** name 10–15 Turkish end users or OEMs per target, state the likely applications, and define what Atlas would do in the first 90 days.
4. **Start with seals and filters:** send no messages until channel checks are complete; then approach SPETECH, Erving and SEDA first. Use Roten and Kingtron as the second pair based on responses and sector pull.
5. **Qualification gate for a meeting:** confirm no exclusive Turkey partner, willingness to use commission/representation/back-to-back trade, target sectors, price-list/export terms, certification scope, lead times and sample/technical-support policy.

The operational goal for Sprint 002 should be **two qualified manufacturer calls**, not a larger unverified list.
