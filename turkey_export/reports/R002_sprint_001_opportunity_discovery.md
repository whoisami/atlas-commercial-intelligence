## Executive Decision

Sprint 001 identified 20 evidence-backed manufacturer–buyer hypotheses. Eleven clear the preliminary `PROCEED` gate and five are immediate priorities. These are commercial opportunities—not confirmed demand, RFQs, orders or buyer intent.

The highest-probability first action is to qualify Pro-Mak Automotive for a KION Group supplier-development route. Pro-Mak shows serial precision machining, drivetrain/gear capability, European export readiness and a detailed machine park. KION provides open e-source self-registration for future tenders and RFQs, including Turkish-language supplier governance. Atlas could earn through an export commission or later representation agreement, but no rate or revenue value is verified.

## Method

### Manufacturer selection

Six Turkish manufacturers were reviewed using official capability, quality, machinery, export-readiness and contact evidence. Five passed Discovery Engine hard gates; Smartist remains `WAIT` because direct export evidence is not verified.

### Buyer selection

Ten foreign OEM or Tier supplier organizations were retained only where official sources showed production-component demand, supplier governance, procurement activity or a supplier-entry mechanism. A supplier portal proves access architecture—not current demand for a specific part.

### Opportunity scoring

The Match Score is a 0–100 weighted decision score:

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Manufacturer export readiness | 20 |
| Product/capability fit | 25 |
| Buyer procurement signal | 20 |
| Contactability / entry route | 10 |
| 90-day first-meeting feasibility | 15 |
| Atlas commercial-model fit | 10 |
| **Total** | **100** |

`PROCEED` requires Match Score at least 75, a `PROCEED` manufacturer, buyer need/procurement evidence, a plausible Atlas model and a defined first action. `WAIT` means a decisive fit, channel, qualification or access fact is missing. `DROP` means evidence disproves fit or risk makes further effort uneconomic.

Commercial-model scores measure suitability only. They are not revenue forecasts. Estimated order and commission potential remain `UNKNOWN` because no RFQ, price, volume, commission rate or buyer mandate is public.

## Top 5 PROCEED Opportunities

### 1. Pro-Mak → KION Group

- **Why this match:** Serial precision machining; broaching and gear production align with industrial-vehicle direct components.
- **Why now:** KION's e-source portal explicitly enables self-registration for future tenders and RFQs.
- **Atlas economics:** Export commission is the best initial model. Rate and potential are `UNKNOWN` until Pro-Mak agrees terms and a part/RFQ exists.
- **Gate:** Pro-Mak must confirm Atlas mandate; available capacity; certificate validity and absence of KION channel conflict.

### 2. ERBAB → CNH Industrial

- **Why this match:** ERBAB's serial precision machining; European automotive export and quality systems match CNH's supplier screening fields.
- **Why now:** CNH maintains a live prospective-supplier application reviewed by the global Purchasing community.
- **Atlas economics:** Export commission; with representation considered only after a validated buyer response.
- **Gate:** Confirm DUNS readiness; current certificates; capacity and non-conflict with CNH brands.

### 3. Kurutlu → GEA

- **Why this match:** Kurutlu's flexible 3–5 axis capacity maps directly to GEA's stated need for subcontracted manufacturing.
- **Why now:** GEA publishes both the need and a supplier-registration route.
- **Atlas economics:** Sourcing partnership or export commission. Financial potential is `UNKNOWN`.
- **Gate:** Verify actual export references; machine availability and material/tolerance envelope.

### 4. Ulus Metal → Solaris

- **Why this match:** Sheet-metal forming; welding and assembly fit bus component production.
- **Why now:** Solaris's current supplier handbook evidences a formal procurement process.
- **Atlas economics:** Export commission is plausible only if Ulus's Polish presence does not already cover Solaris.
- **Gate:** Channel-conflict verification is mandatory; bypass risk is the highest among the Top 5.

### 5. Girginer → Škoda Group

- **Why this match:** Forging; machining and coating support serial transport components.
- **Why now:** Škoda has active 2026 vehicle orders and production procurement.
- **Atlas economics:** Long-term export representation may fit but only after technical qualification.
- **Gate:** Verify quality certificates; rail applicability; public commercial contact and procurement access.

## Key Risks

- No buyer has published an RFQ for the exact matched component.
- Supplier portals do not guarantee a meeting or tender invitation.
- Manufacturer capacity; pricing; margins and commercial openness are unverified.
- Existing buyer relationships may create channel conflict or bypass Atlas.
- Certifications are largely company-stated and need current certificate documents.
- Automotive and rail qualification may exceed the 90-day revenue target.
- Estimated order and commission potential cannot be calculated without parts; volumes; prices and terms.
- Turkey/EU customs and rules-of-origin effects were not priced in this sprint.
