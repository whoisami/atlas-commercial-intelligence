# Turkey Export Opportunity Playbook

## Mission

Help one Turkish OEM metal-components manufacturer decide where, whom and how to approach for qualified foreign export business.

## Evidence rules

- Never invent capabilities, certifications, capacity, customers, contacts, demand or order values.
- Cite a source URL or supplied company record for every decision-critical claim.
- Use `UNKNOWN` when evidence is unavailable.
- Distinguish manufacturer claims from independently verified evidence.
- Use public business information only; do not collect private personal data.
- Do not contact any buyer without explicit authorization.

## Eligible products

Only analyze:

- CNC machining
- Sheet metal
- Precision machining
- Metal fabrication
- Welded assemblies

Reject opportunities outside OEM Metal Components until module scope is formally changed.

## Eligible countries

Initial country selection is limited to Germany, Netherlands, Poland, Italy and Czechia.

## Workflow and gates

### 1. Manufacturer profile

Use `templates/manufacturer_profile.md`. Confirm legal identity, actual production, product categories, materials, processes, machinery, tolerances, capacity, quality systems, certifications, current export experience, languages, logistics, target order profile and commercial constraints.

### 2. Export readiness check

Score each factor 0–100 and record evidence:

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Manufacturing and technical capability | 20 |
| Quality systems and certification fit | 15 |
| Capacity and delivery reliability | 15 |
| Export and logistics capability | 15 |
| Commercial responsiveness | 10 |
| Documentation and language readiness | 10 |
| Target-market product fit | 15 |

`Export Readiness Score = weighted average of available verified factors.` Missing critical evidence cannot earn points and must be listed as a risk.

- **PROCEED:** score at least 75 and no critical capability, quality, capacity or compliance gap
- **WAIT:** score 50–74 or a decision-critical fact is missing
- **DROP:** score below 50 or a material disqualifier is verified

### 3. Target country selection

Run the Country Selector. Compare only eligible countries using product demand evidence, buyer availability, sourcing compatibility, competition, logistics, standards, payment risk and first-meeting feasibility. Select one best country; ties remain `WAIT` until the decisive evidence is obtained.

### 4. Foreign buyer discovery

Run the Buyer Finder. Identify companies with evidence of buying, specifying or integrating outsourced OEM metal components. Reject directories, marketplaces, consultants, unrelated distributors and unverifiable companies.

Return up to 20 qualified potential buyers. If fewer qualify, report fewer and explain the evidence gap.

### 5. Buyer scoring

Run the Buyer Scorer for every candidate. Produce a comparable 0–100 score, key evidence, risks and priority. Select the Top 5 only from verified candidates.

### 6. Outreach drafting

Run the Outreach Writer for each priority buyer. Draft—not send—one evidence-based email connecting the manufacturer's verified capability to the buyer's likely sourcing need. Do not claim an existing relationship, guaranteed savings, capacity or certification that was not verified.

### 7. Meeting tracking

Use `templates/meeting_tracker.md` for authorized outreach outcomes, follow-ups, meetings, objections, commitments and next actions. No reply is not failure until the approved follow-up and observation period are complete.

### 8. Decision

Every opportunity ends with one decision:

- **PROCEED:** export readiness is sufficient, one country is selected, qualified buyers exist and a controlled next action is ready.
- **WAIT:** the opportunity remains plausible but named evidence or readiness work is required.
- **DROP:** fit, readiness, economics, risk or buyer evidence does not justify further effort.

## Required opportunity output

1. Turkish manufacturer summary
2. Export readiness score with factor evidence
3. Best target country and selection rationale
4. Top 20 potential foreign buyers
5. Top 5 priority buyers
6. Buyer fit score for every listed buyer
7. Outreach email draft for each priority buyer
8. Key risks
9. Next best action, owner and due date
10. Decision: `PROCEED` / `WAIT` / `DROP`

## Stop rules

Stop when a decision can be made. Do not add low-fit buyers to fill a quota. Pause on sanctions, compliance, fraud, confidentiality, capacity or material product-quality concerns. Escalate any action involving external contact or commercial commitment.
