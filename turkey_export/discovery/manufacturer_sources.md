# Manufacturer Sources

## Source hierarchy

### Tier 1 — Primary company evidence

- Official company websites
- Technical catalogues and capability documents
- Official certification or quality pages
- Official LinkedIn company pages

Use these sources for identity, manufacturing capabilities, machinery claims, certifications, contact channels and company-stated export activity.

### Tier 2 — Institutional discovery and corroboration

- Organized Industrial Zone (`OSB`) directories
- Sector association member directories
- Trade fair exhibitor lists
- Chamber of industry records
- Exporters' union directories
- ISO or quality-certification references from issuing or accredited sources

Use these sources to discover candidates and corroborate location, sector, membership, exhibition, export orientation or certification. Institutional listing does not by itself prove manufacturing capability.

### Tier 3 — Secondary discovery sources

- B2B directories
- Reputable industrial directories
- Search-engine results leading to primary sources

Use Tier 3 only to find candidates or additional evidence routes. Marketplaces, scraped profiles and search snippets are never final evidence.

## Source-class search plan

| Source class | Discovery purpose | Evidence it may support | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| OSB directories | Find industrial companies by region | Location and industrial presence | Stale or broad classifications |
| Sector associations | Find specialized members | Sector alignment and membership | Membership is not factory evidence |
| Trade fair exhibitor lists | Find market-active suppliers | Product positioning and international activity | Exhibitor may be distributor |
| Chamber records | Confirm industrial identity | Company and sector record | Limited technical detail |
| Export union directories | Find export-oriented candidates | Export-readiness signal | Listing may lack current activity |
| Company websites | Verify the manufacturer | Capabilities, factory, machinery, contacts | Self-reported claims |
| LinkedIn company pages | Corroborate operating presence | Team, language and size signals | Self-reported or incomplete |
| B2B directories | Expand the candidate universe | Initial product classification | Duplication and low trust |
| Certification references | Verify quality claims | Certificate scope and validity | Unverified logos or expired certificates |

## Evidence recording rules

- Store direct page URLs, separated by `|` in CSV.
- Prefer the page supporting the claim, not a search-results page.
- Record `UNKNOWN` when a claimed fact cannot be verified.
- Treat certification logos as claims until scope and validity can be checked.
- Treat apparent company size as an estimate and explain its basis.
- Never use private personal information.
