## Why exports are not growing faster

- **Certification-capability/order mismatch:** ERBAB holds AS9100 and a NATO facility security certificate — both defense/aerospace-grade credentials — but has no independently evidenced current defense export order. The certification appears to be a dormant or underused asset rather than an active revenue line.
- **Automotive concentration:** ERBAB's most detailed, repeated evidence is automotive Tier-2 supply (Tofaş/Fiat, and by extension Ford/Renault/Opel/Mercedes/BMW/Porsche) — a commoditized, already-served channel with limited differentiation and, per prior Atlas briefs (`briefs/COB_001_Germany_OEM_Machining/`), weak fresh demand-side signal in that specific channel.
- **No evidenced business-development function:** no public job posting, hiring signal or partner/agent search was found for ERBAB in this or prior briefs — consistent with a company that is technically capable but has no visible export-sales expansion capacity of its own.
- **Cluster membership without evidenced conversion:** SAHA İstanbul membership grants access to matchmaking infrastructure (SAHA EXPO, NATO DIANA/NIF-aligned meetings), but no evidence in this brief shows ERBAB has used that access to convert a defense-adjacent lead into an order.

## Export Growth Gaps

1. No confirmed subcontract relationship with a Turkish defense prime (Roketsan, Aselsan, or similar) whose end products are exported to Europe or the Gulf — the most realistic near-term export-multiplier path for a Tier-2-sized manufacturer (see `opportunity_analysis.md`).
2. No fresh (post-2019) dated defense-sector signal — the NATO facility security certificate is the most recent dated defense fact on record.
3. No evidenced export-sales or business-development headcount or search activity.
4. Machine-park and capacity figures are contradictory between sources — unresolved for capacity planning with any buyer.

## European / Middle East market fit

- **Middle East (primary fit):** Gulf-state ammunition/component shortages and Turkey-Gulf defense-industrial partnership growth (see `commercial_opportunity_brief.md`) align directly with ERBAB's SAHA-listed ammunition-component and precision-machining capability.
- **Europe (secondary fit):** NATO-ally supply-chain diversification toward Turkish defense firms is real at the policy level, but named European primes have not been identified as actively subcontracting ammunition-adjacent machining to Turkish Tier-2 suppliers within this brief's evidence.

## Buyer accessibility

Direct accessibility to Gulf ministries of defense or European primes is low for a company of ERBAB's size — these buyers transact through qualified prime contractors, not open subcontractor sourcing. The realistic accessible layer is one step removed: Turkish primes with existing Gulf/European export programs, and SAHA İstanbul's cluster-level matchmaking. See `opportunity_analysis.md`.
