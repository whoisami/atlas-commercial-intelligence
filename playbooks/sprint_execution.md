# Sprint Execution Playbook

## Purpose

This is the mandatory operational execution manual for every Atlas Commercial Intelligence sprint. It converts one verified commercial opportunity into one focused attempt to obtain the first manufacturer meeting.

Atlas does not run multiple unrelated opportunities inside one sprint. A new opportunity starts only after the current sprint is completed, terminated or formally moved to WATCH by Atlas CEO.

## Sprint objective

Every sprint has:

- **one commercial opportunity**;
- **one product family**;
- **one primary commercial objective**;
- **one accountable sprint owner**.

The maximum initial objective is to obtain the first qualified manufacturer meeting. The sprint must not expand into broad portfolio research, speculative inventory, negotiation of exclusivity or unrelated product families.

## Required sprint control record

At sprint opening, record the following in `workspace/current_sprint.md` and keep it current:

| Field | Required value |
|---|---|
| Sprint Goal | One measurable commercial outcome |
| Sprint Scope | Included product, geography, manufacturer profile and permitted work |
| Current Opportunity | Stable opportunity name or ID |
| Current Product Family | One product family only |
| Target Manufacturers | Discovery target and approved outreach shortlist |
| Target Buyers | Discovery target and priority demand evidence |
| Commercial Model | Representation, commission, back-to-back, hybrid or UNKNOWN |
| Sprint KPIs | Counts, rates, deadlines and owners |
| Exit Criteria | GO completion, WATCH pause and NO-GO termination conditions |

Unsupported fields are `UNKNOWN`; they are never silently assumed.

## Mandatory execution flow

```text
Opportunity Selection

                            │ viable

             execute   verify   terminate
```

A phase does not begin until its required upstream gate is recorded. GO permits only the next stated phase. WATCH pauses progression until the named evidence is resolved. NO-GO terminates the current path.

## Phase 1 — Opportunity Selection

**Run:** 01 Market Opportunity

**Purpose:** Select one profitable, repeat-purchase, technical B2B product family relevant to Turkey and compatible with representation, commission or back-to-back trade.

**Required output:** Evidence-backed opportunity report and GO / WATCH / NO-GO decision.

**Gate:**

- **GO:** Lock the selected opportunity and product family for this sprint; continue to Phase 2.
- **WATCH:** Verify only the named decision-critical evidence; do not progress.
- **NO-GO:** Close the candidate and return to Opportunity Selection with a different opportunity.

## Phase 2 — Product Intelligence

**Run:** 02 Product Intelligence

**Purpose:** Validate technical structure, applications, buying logic, replacement behavior, stock-free feasibility, support requirements and commercial suitability.

**Required output:** Product intelligence report with GO / WATCH / NO-GO and a commercial recommendation.

**Gate:**

- **GO:** Continue to Phase 3.
- **WATCH:** Resolve the stated product or commercial unknown.
- **NO-GO:** Terminate the opportunity and return to Phase 1.

## Phase 3 — Manufacturer Discovery

**Run:** 03 Manufacturer Hunter

**Target:** 20–30 qualified manufacturers. Quality takes priority over filling the range.

**Purpose:** Identify real, export-capable manufacturers with credible technical products and a realistic probability of accepting Turkish market development, representation, commission or back-to-back cooperation.

**Required output:** Evidence-backed manufacturer records, Top 10, Top 5 priority targets and Top 3 immediate candidates.

**Reject:** Distributors, traders, marketplaces, unverifiable manufacturers, companies without export capability and manufacturers already deeply established in Turkey.

**Gate:** Advance evidence-backed candidates to Phase 4. Do not contact them.

## Phase 4 — Manufacturer Validation

**Run:** 04 Manufacturer Due Diligence

**Goal:** Approve a maximum shortlist of five high-confidence manufacturers.

**Purpose:** Verify legal existence, factory and product evidence, export capability, commercial stability, certifications, response channels, Turkey presence, channel conflict, sanctions and material risks before contact.

**Required output:** Individual GO / WATCH / NO-GO decisions with verification gaps and recommended next action.

**Gate:**

- Only GO manufacturers enter outreach preparation.
- WATCH manufacturers remain outside outreach until the named gap is resolved.
- NO-GO manufacturers are rejected and replaced only from the Phase 3 qualified pool.

## Phase 5 — Buyer Discovery

**Run:** 05 Buyer Intelligence

**Target:** 50–100 qualified Turkish buyers. Never pad the list to meet the range.

**Purpose:** Confirm that real Turkish buyer segments, use cases and commercial demand exist before Atlas approaches a manufacturer.

**Required output:** Evidence-backed buyer pipeline, Top 25 priority buyers and Top 10 immediate targets, with product-need rationale and buying-role hypotheses clearly labeled.

**Gate:** Buyer evidence must be sufficient to support the manufacturer value proposition. No buyer outreach is authorized by this phase.

## Phase 6 — Commercial Economics

**Run:**

- 06 Pricing Intelligence
- 07 Competition Intelligence

These analyses may use the same locked opportunity evidence, but both must finish before Phase 7.

**Purpose:** Validate landed economics, margin or commission potential, capital exposure, pricing risk, competition, market-entry barriers and the appropriate commercial model.

**Required outputs:**

- recommended commercial model;
- verified, estimated and unknown economic inputs;
- market-entry strategy;
- commercial viability and GO / WATCH / NO-GO decisions;
- major risks and next verification action.

**Gate:**

- Continue only when commercial viability is supported and no unresolved hard-stop risk remains.
- WATCH triggers a bounded verification task.
- NO-GO terminates the sprint.

## Phase 7 — Commercial Outreach Preparation

**Run:** 08 Outreach Intelligence

**Purpose:** Convert verified intelligence into a manufacturer-specific meeting-conversion package.

**Prepare for each approved manufacturer:**

- personalized outreach package;
- meeting strategy;
- evidence-backed value proposition;
- objection-handling notes;
- 90-day pilot plan;
- contact sequence, follow-up schedule and stop rules;
- CRM-ready owner, dates and completion evidence.

**Gate:** A GO means ready for CEO authorization. It does not mean sent.

## Phase 8 — CEO Decision

**Run:** 09 Atlas CEO

Atlas CEO reads accepted outputs and decides GO, WATCH or NO-GO. Atlas CEO does not perform missing research.

**Decision:**

- **GO:** Approve a named outreach package, manufacturer list, channel, owner and execution window.
- **WATCH:** Request the minimum missing evidence, assign an owner and set a review trigger. Outreach remains paused.
- **NO-GO:** Terminate the sprint, record the reason and identify whether Atlas should return to Phase 1.

No verified opportunity means no outreach. No explicit CEO approval means no send, connection request or commercial contact.

## Commercial execution after CEO GO

The authorized owner executes only the approved scope:

1. Send the personalized email.
2. Connect on LinkedIn when included in the approved sequence.
3. Execute the dated follow-up schedule.
4. Stop immediately on opt-out, wrong contact, material contradiction or risk trigger.
5. Book the manufacturer meeting.
6. Conduct the meeting using the approved meeting strategy.
7. Capture meeting notes, objections, commitments, unknowns, owner and next-action date.
8. Update the CRM and dashboard using verified outcomes.

Sending, LinkedIn activity and external commitments require a human or system explicitly authorized by Atlas CEO. Intelligence preparation alone never authorizes contact.

## Post-meeting learning

After a manufacturer reply, meeting or other material outcome, run 10 Learning Intelligence.

Capture:

- lessons learned;
- objections and competing explanations;
- successful and failed messaging signals;
- manufacturer and buyer behavior within the observed cohort;
- next actions and owners;
- recommended commercial or playbook improvements;
- evidence state and confidence.

No reply is an observed outcome only after delivery is verified and the approved follow-up and observation window are complete. It is not automatically sprint failure.

## Sprint KPIs

Every KPI requires a source, owner, reporting window and—when it is a rate—a numerator and denominator.

| KPI | Minimum definition |
|---|---|
| Opportunity gate | Recorded Phase 1 status |
| Product gate | Recorded Phase 2 status |
| Qualified manufacturers | Count clearing Phase 3 evidence rules |
| Due-diligence GO manufacturers | Count clearing Phase 4 hard gates; maximum shortlist 5 |
| Qualified Turkish buyers | Count with evidence-backed product-need rationale |
| Commercial viability | Pricing and Competition decisions recorded |
| Outreach-ready manufacturers | Count with complete manufacturer-specific package |
| Authorized contacts | Count explicitly approved by Atlas CEO |
| Valid contacts reached | Delivered contacts excluding verified bounces or invalid routes |
| Human replies | Unique valid contacts producing a human reply |
| Qualified meetings booked | Meetings with purpose, relevant participants and next step recorded |
| Follow-up completion | Approved follow-ups completed / approved follow-ups due |
| Learning completion | Material outcomes with evidence-backed learning capture / material outcomes due for review |

Targets belong in the sprint control record and must not be invented.

## Sprint success criteria

A sprint is commercially successful when at least one verified event occurs:

- a manufacturer meeting is booked;
- a manufacturer requests more information;
- a manufacturer requests pricing;
- a manufacturer requests a second meeting;
- a manufacturer agrees to a pilot.

Record the evidence and date. A positive signal does not authorize commitments outside the CEO-approved scope.

## Sprint failure and learning discipline

No reply alone is not failure.

A sprint has failed operationally when any of the following remains true at closure:

- no learning was captured from completed activity;
- an approved follow-up was not executed or explicitly cancelled with reason;
- no GO / WATCH / NO-GO decision was made;
- the sprint drifted into unrelated opportunities without formal closure.

Commercial rejection can still produce a successful learning sprint when the process was completed, the decision was made and the evidence improves the next action.

## Exit criteria

### GO completion

Close the sprint when the first qualified manufacturer meeting is booked or another defined success event is verified, records are synchronized and Learning Intelligence has captured the material outcome.

### WATCH pause

Pause only with a named missing fact, owner, due date, review trigger and consequence if unresolved. WATCH is not indefinite research.

### NO-GO termination

Terminate when a hard gate fails, economics are unacceptable, buyer demand is unsupported, risk is material, or Atlas CEO decides expected commercial value no longer justifies effort. Record the reason and next opportunity action.

## Atlas operating principles

1. One sprint.
2. One product family.
3. One commercial objective.
4. Evidence before action.
5. Execution before perfection.
6. Revenue before complexity.
7. No verified opportunity, no outreach.
8. Commercial decisions over beautiful reports.

## Sprint closure checklist

- [ ] Sprint Goal, Scope, Opportunity and Product Family are recorded.
- [ ] Every required phase has a traceable output and decision.
- [ ] Target Manufacturers and Target Buyers reflect verified records.
- [ ] Commercial Model and economics are explicit or `UNKNOWN` with a gate.
- [ ] CEO decision and outreach authorization scope are recorded.
- [ ] CRM, dashboard and current sprint records match observed outcomes.
- [ ] Follow-ups are completed, scheduled or cancelled with reason.
- [ ] Learning Intelligence reviewed every material outcome.
- [ ] Sprint KPIs and Exit Criteria are evaluated.
- [ ] Sprint is formally completed, paused under WATCH or terminated under NO-GO.
