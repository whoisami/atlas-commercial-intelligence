# Evidence and Verification Playbook

## Verification sequence

1. Establish legal or trading identity.
2. Confirm the entity performs the claimed role.
3. Confirm product or market evidence.
4. Confirm international activity where relevant.
5. Check dates and certificate validity.
6. Search for conflicts, exclusions and contradictory evidence.
7. Record unresolved facts as `UNKNOWN`.
8. Assign evidence level E1–E4.
9. Make the narrowest defensible recommendation.

## Minimum source rules

- Manufacturer: official production evidence plus one technical or certification source.
- Buyer: official operating evidence plus a relevant facility, process, procurement or import signal.
- Price: product, unit, currency, Incoterm, date and source must be known.
- Contact: name, title and email must each be verified independently or marked `UNKNOWN`.
- Distributor claim: only an official manufacturer or distributor statement proves authorization.

## Prohibited practices

- Inferring emails from patterns.
- Treating a reseller as an authorized distributor without first-party proof.
- Presenting employee estimates as audited facts.
- Copying marketplace claims into canonical records without verification.
- Converting absence of search results into proof of absence.
