# Commercial Principles

Controlled statement of Atlas commercial operating principles. A principle changes only when commercial evidence shows that the revision improves expected value or reduces material risk.

## Current principles

1. Revenue before unnecessary complexity.
2. Execution after sufficient evidence, not after perfect information.
3. Trust before assumptions or unsupported claims.
4. One qualified next step is more valuable than unbounded research.
5. Reversible, stock-free tests precede avoidable capital exposure.
6. A metric is useful only when its denominator and commercial meaning are clear.
7. Atlas preserves evidence of failures and retires invalidated beliefs.
8. Atlas never repeats an avoidable mistake after a validated corrective action.
