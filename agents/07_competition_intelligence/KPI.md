# KPIs — Competition Intelligence

## North-star outcome

**Share of GO market-entry recommendations that produce a qualified first customer opportunity within the forecast timeline without a preventable competitive surprise.**

The agent is measured on entry-decision accuracy, not competitor count.

## Market-structure KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---:|
| Competitive-arena precision | Reports with explicit product, application, buyer and alternative boundaries | 100% |
| Market-size discipline | Import, domestic production and addressable market not conflated | 100% |
| Channel-verification coverage | Material authorization claims verified first party | 100% |
| Local-manufacturing verification | Relevant local producers classified correctly | ≥ 95% |
| Buyer-alternative coverage | Direct, substitute, in-house and do-nothing alternatives covered | 100% |
| Evidence-state coverage | Material claims labeled VERIFIED/ESTIMATED/UNKNOWN | 100% |

## Entry-decision KPIs

- GO cases with Atlas Score ≥ 75: **100%**
- GO cases with Market Attractiveness ≥ 70: **100%**
- GO cases with Differentiation Opportunity ≥ 60: **100%**
- GO cases with Profitability ≥ 60: **100%**
- GO cases with Competition Intensity ≤ 70: **100%**
- GO cases with Entry Difficulty ≤ 65: **100%**
- GO cases with Evidence Quality ≥ 70: **100%**
- Score arithmetic errors: **0**

## Commercial outcome KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---:|
| First-customer timing accuracy | Forecast versus actual qualified first-customer opportunity | Track by cohort; improve quarterly |
| Competitive-surprise rate | Material public competitor/channel fact missed before GO | ≤ 5% |
| Differentiation validation | Proposed differentiation confirmed by buyer/manufacturer feedback | ≥ 70% |
| Entry-strategy persistence | Recommended strategy retained after first commercial validation | ≥ 75% |
| Quick-win realization | Recommended quick wins producing meetings, evidence or revenue | Track by sprint |
| False-GO rate | GO later rejected for evidence available at decision time | ≤ 10% |

## Strategy-quality KPIs

- Reports comparing all six entry models: **100%**
- GO recommendations with one primary strategy and explicit win condition: **100%**
- Stock-model recommendations without inventory economics: **0**
- Generic SWOT items: **0**
- Entry barriers without mitigation or explicit unresolved status: **0**

## Research-efficiency KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---:|
| Time to market-entry decision | Complete upstream evidence to GO/WATCH/NO-GO | ≤ 7 business days unless data access blocks |
| Low-value competitor counting | Competitors added without decision relevance | 0 |
| Open-ended WATCH | WATCH without evidence request, owner and trigger | 0 |
| Duplicate channel research | Rework caused by missing citations/classification | 0 |
| Monitoring actionability | Watch triggers tied to a decision or owner | 100% |

## Guardrails

- Invented market, growth, share, price, loyalty or channel claims: **0**
- Authorization inferred from reseller pages: **0**
- Import value mislabeled as market size: **0**
- Deceptive competitor contact: **0**
- Private information collected: **0**
- Unauthorized contact, stock, spending or agreement: **0**
- Other agents' files or metrics changed: **0**

## Feedback loop

Atlas CEO, Outreach Intelligence and Buyer Intelligence record:

- Buyer response to differentiation.
- Incumbents and channels discovered in conversations.
- Actual sales-cycle and switching barriers.
- Service and stock expectations.
- Reasons entry strategy changed.
- First-customer timing.
- Quick-win outcomes.

Learning Intelligence uses these outcomes to recalibrate scoring and monitoring triggers.

## Review cadence

- Validate GO gates before report closure.
- Review watch triggers monthly.
- Compare forecast to first-customer outcomes after commercial validation.
- Recalibrate by product family and buyer segment quarterly.
- Never improve apparent attractiveness by suppressing competition evidence.
