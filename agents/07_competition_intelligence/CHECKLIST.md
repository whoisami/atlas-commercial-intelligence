# Checklist — Competition Intelligence

## Start gate

- [ ] Market Opportunity report is accepted.
- [ ] Product Intelligence report is accepted.
- [ ] Buyer Intelligence report is accepted.
- [ ] Pricing Intelligence is available or its absence is stated.
- [ ] Product, application, buyer and geography boundaries are explicit.
- [ ] Intended entry decision is explicit.
- [ ] Direct, indirect, substitute, in-house and do-nothing alternatives are defined.

## Evidence states

- [ ] Every material claim is VERIFIED, ESTIMATED or UNKNOWN.
- [ ] VERIFIED claims have attributable sources.
- [ ] ESTIMATED claims show method, date, range and confidence.
- [ ] UNKNOWN claims state impact and next verification.
- [ ] No market claim was invented.
- [ ] Contradictions are preserved.

## Market structure

- [ ] Import value and volume use defensible product/HS scope.
- [ ] Import dependency is not confused with market size.
- [ ] Domestic production and exports are considered.
- [ ] Addressable, serviceable and achievable opportunity are separated.
- [ ] Local manufacturers are verified.
- [ ] Assembly, private label, distribution and own production are separated.
- [ ] Growth claims are sourced or UNKNOWN.
- [ ] Regional demand is tied to facilities/applications, not company count.

## Competitors and channels

- [ ] Major international brands are relevant to the defined arena.
- [ ] Turkey subsidiaries and offices are verified.
- [ ] Authorized distributors are verified first party.
- [ ] Representations are verified first party.
- [ ] Independent resellers are labeled correctly.
- [ ] System-integrator and EPC influence is assessed.
- [ ] Market share is not inferred from visibility.
- [ ] Authorization is not inferred from a seller page.

## Buyer alternatives and behavior

- [ ] Direct competitors are mapped.
- [ ] Substitute technologies are mapped.
- [ ] Repair/refurbishment and in-house options are mapped.
- [ ] Do-nothing alternative is considered.
- [ ] Typical buying behavior is evidenced.
- [ ] Specifier and technical approval influence are mapped.
- [ ] Customer loyalty is evidenced or UNKNOWN.
- [ ] Approved-vendor/framework behavior is assessed.
- [ ] Sales cycle is evidenced or UNKNOWN.
- [ ] Switching costs are explained.

## Pricing and profit pool

- [ ] Pricing comparisons use equivalent specification and basis.
- [ ] Asking and achieved prices are distinguished.
- [ ] Distributor, service, credit and tender pressure are considered.
- [ ] Profit pools are identified with evidence.
- [ ] Margin-compression and price-war risk are assessed.

## Barriers and support

- [ ] Regulation and certification barriers are assessed.
- [ ] Vendor registration and local-reference barriers are assessed.
- [ ] Channel exclusivity is assessed.
- [ ] Stock and lead-time expectations are assessed.
- [ ] Service expectations are assessed.
- [ ] Technical-support requirements are assessed.
- [ ] Credit/payment and language barriers are assessed.
- [ ] Each material barrier has a mitigation or is unresolved.

## Entry models

- [ ] Greenfield entry is analyzed.
- [ ] Representation model is analyzed.
- [ ] Commission model is analyzed.
- [ ] Hybrid model is analyzed.
- [ ] Back-to-back model is analyzed.
- [ ] Stock model is analyzed.
- [ ] Customer and manufacturer value are defined for each.
- [ ] Capital, speed, requirements and risks are defined.
- [ ] Recommended strategy has a specific win condition.
- [ ] Stock is not assumed to be necessary without evidence.

## SWOT

- [ ] Strengths are internal and evidence-linked.
- [ ] Weaknesses are internal and evidence-linked.
- [ ] Opportunities are external and evidence-linked.
- [ ] Threats are external and evidence-linked.
- [ ] Generic statements are excluded.

## Scoring

- [ ] Competition Intensity is scored 0–100, high = severe.
- [ ] Entry Difficulty is scored 0–100, high = severe.
- [ ] Market Attractiveness is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Growth Potential is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Differentiation Opportunity is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Profitability is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Inverse burden scores are correct.
- [ ] Weights total 100%.
- [ ] Atlas Score arithmetic is verified.
- [ ] Evidence Quality is separate.
- [ ] Every score has evidence, confidence and sensitivity.

## GO gate

- [ ] Atlas Score ≥ 75.
- [ ] Market Attractiveness ≥ 70.
- [ ] Differentiation Opportunity ≥ 60.
- [ ] Profitability ≥ 60.
- [ ] Competition Intensity ≤ 70.
- [ ] Entry Difficulty ≤ 65.
- [ ] Evidence Quality ≥ 70.
- [ ] At least one entry strategy is viable.
- [ ] A specific win condition exists.
- [ ] No hidden decision-critical UNKNOWN remains.

If any box fails, GO is prohibited.

## Outputs

- [ ] Report uses the next `intelligence/competition/RXXX.md`.
- [ ] Report ends with the six mandatory sections in order.
- [ ] `database/products.csv` matches the report.
- [ ] `database/manufacturers.csv` preserves discovery/diligence fields.
- [ ] `workspace/active_project.md` matches strategy and status.
- [ ] Only Competition Intelligence dashboard values were updated.
- [ ] Stable IDs, prior evidence and unrelated content are preserved.
- [ ] Report ID and verification date appear in every updated surface.
- [ ] No contact, stock, spend or agreement was authorized.

## Integrity

- [ ] No market size, growth, share, price, loyalty, channel or demand claim was invented.
- [ ] Every recommendation is explained.
- [ ] Private information was not collected.
- [ ] Deceptive or pretextual competitor contact was not used.
