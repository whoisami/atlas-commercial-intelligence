# SOP — Manufacturer Due Diligence

## 1. Confirm the pre-contact gate

1. Read the Manufacturer Hunter report and candidate evidence packet.
2. Confirm the candidate is GO for due diligence.
3. Define the exact proposed next contact action.
4. Confirm the Product Intelligence scope and manufacturer-fit requirements.
5. List every claim that must be independently re-verified.
6. If the handoff lacks a basic identity, manufacturing, export, Turkey-channel or contact claim, assign WATCH or return it.

## 2. Create the entity-resolution record

1. Verify legal name, trading names, jurisdiction and registration number.
2. Verify registered and operating addresses.
3. Identify owners, parent, subsidiaries and group relationships.
4. Match website domain, email domain, phone and addresses to the entity.
5. Resolve similarly named companies.
6. Record contradictions and potential impersonation.

No other verification is reliable until entity resolution is complete.

## 3. Verify legal existence and operating history

1. Check official company registries or authoritative equivalents.
2. Verify active status and filing continuity.
3. Verify year founded separately from legal incorporation where they differ.
4. Verify employee estimate from a defensible source and label the range.
5. Check insolvency, dissolution or material filing warnings where available.
6. Record source limitations by jurisdiction.

## 4. Re-verify manufacturing and factory evidence

1. Re-open first-party production and factory sources.
2. Confirm factory address belongs to the resolved entity.
3. Verify manufacturing processes, equipment, testing and quality capability.
4. Separate owned production, subcontracted production, assembly and resale.
5. Corroborate factory existence independently where feasible.
6. Verify capacity claims only when sourced.
7. Reject false, copied or materially inconsistent manufacturing evidence.

## 5. Verify product consistency

1. Compare website, catalogue, certificate scope, manuals and product claims.
2. Test fit against Product Intelligence requirements.
3. Identify discontinued, white-label or third-party products.
4. Check whether specifications and applications remain consistent across sources.
5. Identify technical claims that require direct confirmation.
6. Record quality, warranty or failure signals.

## 6. Verify certifications

For each material certification:

1. Identify standard, issuer, certificate number, entity, site and scope.
2. Verify current validity where possible.
3. Confirm relevance to the in-scope product.
4. Distinguish management-system certification from product approval.
5. Flag expired, suspended, copied, scope-mismatched or logo-only claims.
6. Determine whether any missing certification blocks Turkey entry.

## 7. Verify export and international capability

1. Re-verify export statements and countries.
2. Confirm international customers only when explicitly sourced.
3. Check foreign subsidiaries, references, trade activity and events.
4. Verify export documentation, language and standards readiness.
5. Evaluate logistics, lead time, returns, warranty and service capability.
6. Verify the export department and official response channels.
7. Reject export capability inferred only from an English website.

## 8. Assess financial credibility and payment risk

1. Search official filings and insolvency records where available.
2. Capture revenue, capital or financial figures only when sourced and dated.
3. Evaluate documented proxies when full statements are unavailable.
4. Identify ownership, concentration and continuity concerns.
5. Assess plausible payment structures and banking friction.
6. Write `UNKNOWN` when creditworthiness cannot be verified.
7. Define controls such as advance payment, documentary terms, credit limits or staged exposure without presenting them as agreed terms.

## 9. Verify contacts and communication quality

1. Verify the company LinkedIn page.
2. Verify export department or international-sales function.
3. Verify named contact, title and email independently.
4. Verify sales/general emails and phone from first-party sources.
5. Assess domain consistency and impersonation risk.
6. Assess clarity and quality of international communication materials.
7. Do not send a message.

## 10. Investigate Turkey presence and representation conflict

1. Search official manufacturer partner/distributor locators.
2. Search Turkey/Türkiye on the manufacturer site.
3. Search Turkish-language sources.
4. Verify any claimed distributor through first-party evidence.
5. Check Turkish offices, subsidiaries, employees, projects, customers and trade fairs.
6. Classify channel status using the controlled categories.
7. Identify direct-sales or multi-distributor behavior that could undermine Atlas.
8. Assign Representation Risk.
9. An unresolved material conflict blocks GO.

## 11. Screen sanctions and trade restrictions

1. Determine the legal entity names, aliases, owners and relevant jurisdictions.
2. Check current authoritative sanctions sources.
3. Resolve potential matches; never dismiss a match by intuition.
4. Check product, end-use, destination, dual-use and export-control questions where relevant.
5. Check country, banking and payment-route restrictions.
6. Record sources, date, names screened and limitations.
7. Escalate any potential match or legal ambiguity.
8. Unresolved material sanctions/trade risk is NO-GO pending qualified review.

## 12. Check reputation and brand risk

1. Search credible adverse media and industry sources.
2. Check documented disputes, quality failures, recalls, fraud allegations or regulatory action.
3. Separate allegation from verified fact.
4. Assess customer-reference credibility.
5. Assess how association could affect Atlas's reputation.
6. Record contrary positive evidence as well.

## 13. Score the nine risks

1. Score Commercial Risk.
2. Score Fraud Risk.
3. Score Supply Risk.
4. Score Communication Risk.
5. Score Political Risk.
6. Score Logistics Risk.
7. Score Payment Risk.
8. Score Representation Risk.
9. Score Brand Risk.
10. Calculate weighted Overall Due Diligence Risk.
11. Score Evidence Quality separately.
12. Provide evidence, confidence, controls and gaps for every score.

A higher raw score always means greater risk.

## 14. Apply the decision framework

- GO only if every numeric and evidence gate passes.
- WATCH when a bounded verification or control could close the gap.
- NO-GO for failed core verification, prohibited risk or structural mismatch.

Do not average away a fatal risk.

## 15. Write and synchronize outputs

1. Write the next `intelligence/manufacturers/RXXX.md`.
2. End with GO/WATCH/NO-GO and the six mandatory commercial-action fields.
3. Update the due-diligence fields in `database/manufacturers.csv`.
4. Update `workspace/active_project.md` with status, controls and next gate.
5. Update only Manufacturer Due Diligence-owned dashboard values.
6. Preserve stable IDs, prior evidence and unrelated content.
7. Cite the report ID and verification date.
8. Route GO to Atlas CEO and Outreach Intelligence for controlled contact preparation.

## Escalation

Immediately escalate sanctions matches, export-control ambiguity, fraud/impersonation, bribery concerns, insolvency, legal claims, unsafe products or any request for a legal/credit conclusion beyond available evidence.

## Completion definition

Diligence is complete only when Atlas can explain why contact is safe, what remains uncertain, which controls are required, who owns the next action and what business impact is expected.
