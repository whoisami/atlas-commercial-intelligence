# Inputs — Buyer Intelligence

## Mandatory upstream handoff

- Accepted `intelligence/market/RXXX.md` with GO
- Accepted `intelligence/products/RXXX.md` with GO
- Selected product family
- Product boundaries and variants
- Applications and industries
- Technical selection criteria
- Buyer-role model
- OEM, project, aftermarket and MRO routes
- Maintenance and repeat-purchase logic
- Stock and remote-support conclusions
- Certification and regulatory constraints
- Product-value proposition

If either upstream report is absent or not GO, stop.

## Public demand sources

Use lawfully available sources, including:

- Official company and facility websites
- Turkish trade and industry associations
- Chambers and industrial-zone records
- Public tenders and procurement notices
- Municipality and public-utility sources
- Investment, expansion and project announcements
- OEM product catalogues
- EPC and system-integrator references
- Maintenance/MRO/service portfolios
- Environmental, safety and operating permits when public and relevant
- Trade-fair exhibitor lists
- Public professional company pages and roles
- Credible industry publications
- Existing internal evidence with source history

## Mandatory buyer fields

Every evaluated buyer must include:

- Company Name
- Industry
- City
- Website
- Buyer Type
- Potential Product Need
- Reason They May Buy
- Estimated Purchase Frequency
- Decision Maker Department
- Purchasing Contact, if public
- LinkedIn
- Email, if public
- Phone
- Source URLs
- Atlas Notes

Use `UNKNOWN` for unverified fields.

## Minimum inputs for GO

- Verified Turkish operation and city.
- Verified buyer type.
- Relevant facility, product or service activity.
- Specific product need.
- Evidence-backed reason to buy.
- Demand classification.
- Purchase route and trigger.
- Decision-maker department.
- Public company response channel.
- Enough evidence for scoring.

## Privacy and evidence rules

- Use public business information only.
- Do not bypass access controls or scrape private data.
- Do not infer personal email patterns or phone numbers.
- Do not collect irrelevant personal data.
- Record contact information only with source URL and verification date.
- Distinguish official company contacts from third-party directory data.
- Mark hypotheses as hypotheses.
- Mark missing data `UNKNOWN` and name the next verification step.
