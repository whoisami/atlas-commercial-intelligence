# Checklist — Product Intelligence

## Start gate

- [ ] An accepted Market Opportunity report exists.
- [ ] The selected product family is GO.
- [ ] The product-family boundary and market thesis are explicit.
- [ ] Product Intelligence is not re-running market selection.
- [ ] No manufacturer shortlist is being built.

## Product expertise

- [ ] Primary function and product boundaries are clear.
- [ ] Components, materials, interfaces and failure points are mapped.
- [ ] Synonyms and ambiguous terminology are resolved.
- [ ] Major variants are classified consistently.
- [ ] Standard versus engineered-to-order products are separated.
- [ ] Minimum selection and quotation parameters are listed.
- [ ] Operating limits, standards and critical tolerances are sourced.
- [ ] Technical mistakes and consequences are identified.

## Applications and buyers

- [ ] Applications are mapped to variants and specifications.
- [ ] Relevant industries and facility types are identified.
- [ ] User, specifier and technical approver are identified or `UNKNOWN`.
- [ ] Budget approver, purchaser, payer and installer are identified or `UNKNOWN`.
- [ ] Purchase triggers are explicit.
- [ ] OEM, project, aftermarket and MRO routes are separated.

## Recurrence and lifecycle

- [ ] Maintenance and wear mechanisms are understood.
- [ ] Replacement triggers are sourced.
- [ ] Repeat-purchase behavior is explained without invented intervals.
- [ ] Lifecycle stages and obsolescence risks are mapped.
- [ ] Switching costs and installed-base effects are considered.

## Order and representation feasibility

- [ ] Order unit and basket logic are clear.
- [ ] Typical order-size evidence is verified or `UNKNOWN`.
- [ ] MOQ evidence is verified or `UNKNOWN`.
- [ ] Lead-time and emergency-demand implications are assessed.
- [ ] Stock requirement is scored and explained.
- [ ] A stock-free order flow is operationally described or rejected.
- [ ] Remote technical support capability is assessed.
- [ ] Local installation, commissioning, service and training needs are bounded.
- [ ] Warranty, returns and failure-analysis responsibilities are clear enough.

## Certification, regulation and classification

- [ ] Mandatory regulation is separated from buyer preference.
- [ ] Certifications are mapped by application and jurisdiction.
- [ ] Documentation, testing and traceability requirements are identified.
- [ ] GTIP/HS candidates are sourced.
- [ ] Classification uncertainty and verification route are explicit.
- [ ] No provisional code is presented as final.

## Competitive product logic

- [ ] Complementary products and consumables are mapped.
- [ ] Direct substitutes and competing technologies are mapped.
- [ ] Value proposition is stated for user, specifier, procurement and payer.
- [ ] The strongest competing technology and switching barrier are addressed.

## Scoring

- [ ] Technical Complexity is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Commercial Complexity is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Sales Cycle is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Margin Potential is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Repeat Purchase is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Stock Requirement is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Training Requirement is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Certification Requirement is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Market Entry Difficulty is scored 0–100.
- [ ] Score direction is not reversed or obscured.
- [ ] Commercial Product Fit arithmetic is verified.
- [ ] Every score has evidence, confidence, interpretation and unknowns.
- [ ] Technical complexity is rewarded only when manageable and differentiating.

## Decision quality

- [ ] GO means the product is ready for manufacturer research.
- [ ] WATCH names the exact verification required and owner.
- [ ] NO-GO states whether rejection is structural or reviewable.
- [ ] No unresolved risk could make the product unsafe, unsellable or incompatible with representation.
- [ ] Commercial recommendation addresses stock-free and remote-support feasibility.
- [ ] The report ends with GO, WATCH or NO-GO and recommendation.

## Output synchronization

- [ ] Report is saved as the next `intelligence/products/RXXX.md`.
- [ ] `database/products.csv` matches the report.
- [ ] `workspace/active_project.md` matches the status and next gate.
- [ ] Only Product Intelligence-owned dashboard values were updated.
- [ ] Stable IDs and unrelated content were preserved.
- [ ] Source report and verification date appear in every updated surface.
- [ ] Manufacturer Hunter handoff is complete enough to avoid guessing.

## Integrity

- [ ] No specifications, codes, certifications, order sizes, MOQs, intervals, prices or companies were invented.
- [ ] Unknowns are explicit and commercially interpreted.
- [ ] Every unknown names what must be verified next.
- [ ] First-party and authoritative technical sources support decisive claims.
- [ ] One manufacturer catalogue was not treated as the universal category definition.
