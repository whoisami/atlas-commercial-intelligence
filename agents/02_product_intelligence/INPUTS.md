# Inputs — Product Intelligence

## Mandatory handoff

- Accepted `intelligence/market/RXXX.md` with a GO product family.
- `workspace/active_project.md`
- `workspace/current_sprint.md`
- `dashboard/CEO_DASHBOARD.md`
- `database/products.csv`
- `database/decisions.csv`

The Market Opportunity report must define the selected family, market thesis, intended commercial model, principal buyer use case, decisive evidence and known unknowns.

If this handoff is absent or not GO, stop.

## Technical evidence inputs

Use the strongest available sources:

- International, European and Turkish standards.
- Turkish regulations and conformity requirements.
- Official tariff schedules, GTIP/HS explanatory notes and customs rulings.
- Technical institutes, engineering handbooks and credible application guides.
- Product datasheets, catalogues, installation manuals and maintenance manuals.
- Testing, certification and notified-body documentation.
- OEM documentation and approved-vendor specifications.
- Tender technical specifications where legally and publicly available.
- Multiple manufacturer catalogues for category understanding only—not manufacturer ranking.
- Failure analyses, service bulletins and lifecycle guidance from credible sources.

## Commercial-product inputs

- `database/pricing.csv` for existing MOQ, lead-time and price-basis evidence.
- `database/buyers.csv` for known buying roles and applications.
- `intelligence/competition/` for competing technologies and incumbent product patterns.
- `intelligence/learning/` for validated product-selection lessons.
- Existing buyer or manufacturer replies when authorized and saved as evidence.

## Required input fields

Before scoring, obtain or mark `UNKNOWN`:

- Functional product definition.
- Variant taxonomy.
- Selection parameters and operating limits.
- Applications and industries.
- Certification and regulatory requirements.
- Buying-center roles.
- OEM versus aftermarket routes.
- Maintenance and replacement mechanisms.
- Order-unit and MOQ evidence.
- Stock and lead-time requirements.
- Installation, training and support needs.
- GTIP/HS-code candidates.
- Complementary and competing technologies.
- Lifecycle and switching behavior.

## Evidence-quality rules

- Verify date, jurisdiction, product scope and units.
- Distinguish mandatory requirements from market convention.
- Distinguish category evidence from a single manufacturer's design.
- Do not treat a catalogue claim as universal.
- Do not infer order size, MOQ, maintenance interval or price.
- Use `UNKNOWN` and name the next authoritative source or test required.
