## Market and trade evidence

Use the strongest available sources, including:

- Turkish official import and production statistics.
- UN Comtrade, ITC Trade Map or equivalent authoritative trade data when available.
- Turkish ministries, regulators and customs/tariff sources.
- Industry associations and official sector reports.
- HS classification sources and explanatory notes.
- Turkish investment, capacity, facility and tender evidence.
- Manufacturer catalogues, export pages and certification records.
- Buyer facility, process, maintenance and procurement evidence.
- Existing `data/import_data/` files when supplied.

## Upstream intelligence

Read accepted, relevant reports from:

- `intelligence/products/`
- `intelligence/manufacturers/`
- `intelligence/buyers/`
- `intelligence/pricing/`
- `intelligence/competition/`
- `intelligence/learning/`

Prior research is evidence, not a predetermined conclusion. Re-score previously studied families using the same criteria as new candidates.

## Minimum inputs for a GO

- Defined product family and application boundary.
- Import-dependency evidence or a clearly labeled, defensible proxy.
- Repeat-purchase mechanism.
- Stock-free order-flow hypothesis.
- Verified pool of plausible manufacturers.
- Verified Turkish buyer segments or accounts.
- Competition and local-production check.
- Timing logic for first meeting and first revenue.
- Evidence Quality score of at least 70.

## Input quality rules

- Confirm units, years, currencies, HS scope and source ownership.
- Do not mix market size with import value.
- Do not treat search snippets as final evidence.
- Do not use unsourced market reports for decisive claims.
- Mark missing exact data `UNKNOWN`.
- Record what must be verified next and whether the gap blocks GO.
