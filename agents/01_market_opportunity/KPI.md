# KPIs — Market Opportunity

## North-star outcome

**Share of selected GO product families that reach a qualified manufacturer or buyer meeting within the planned time window.**

The agent is measured on decision quality and commercial progress, not report length or number of candidates researched.

## Selection-quality KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---:|
| GO validation rate | GO families later confirmed by manufacturer, buyer and pricing work | ≥ 70% |
| False-GO rate | GO families later rejected for evidence available at selection time | ≤ 10% |
| Evidence Quality at GO | Evidence Quality score when GO is issued | ≥ 70 |
| Scoring completeness | Evaluated families with all 11 scores and evidence notes | 100% |
| Reproducible scoring | Scores another reviewer can trace to evidence and rubric | 100% |
| Sector-neutrality | GO result produced without undocumented preference or unequal rules | 100% |

## Commercial-speed KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---:|
| Time to decision | Working time from scoped brief to GO/WATCH/NO-GO | ≤ 5 business days unless data access blocks |
| Time to first qualified meeting | Days from GO to a manufacturer or buyer meeting | Set per report; track actual |
| Time to first revenue | Days from GO to recognized Atlas revenue | Set per report; track actual |
| GO-to-manufacturer-shortlist | Days from GO to five diligence-ready manufacturers | ≤ 10 business days |
| GO-to-buyer-shortlist | Days from GO to ten evidence-backed Turkish buyers | ≤ 10 business days |

## Economic-fit KPIs

- Median Repeat Purchase score of GO selections: **≥ 70**
- Median Stock-Free Feasibility score of GO selections: **≥ 60**
- GO selections with an explicit commission, representation or back-to-back route: **100%**
- GO selections requiring Atlas inventory at launch: **0**

## Research-efficiency KPIs

- Candidate families rejected through fast gates before deep research.
- Hours spent after a product family already satisfied every GO gate.
- Decision-changing unknowns closed per research cycle.
- Reports ending with an executable next step rather than open-ended research.
- Duplicate or low-value research requests avoided.

No target rewards a larger longlist. Research efficiency improves when weak candidates are eliminated early and work stops after a defensible GO.

## Guardrails

- Invented import values, companies, prices or market claims: **0**
- Unsupported numerical precision: **0**
- GO decisions failing any mandatory threshold: **0**
- Product families with missing sources in canonical records: **0**
- Unauthorized outreach, spending, stock or commercial commitments: **0**
- Changes to another agent's dashboard values: **0**

## Review cadence

- Review scoring arithmetic and evidence coverage before every report closes.
- Compare predicted meeting and revenue speed with actuals at sprint close.
- Recalibrate scoring bands quarterly using outcomes recorded by Atlas CEO and Learning Intelligence.
- Retire or revise criteria only through an explicit, documented governance change.
