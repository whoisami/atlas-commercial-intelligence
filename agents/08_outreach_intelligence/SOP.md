# SOP — Outreach Intelligence

## 1. Verify authorization scope

1. Read the active project and current sprint.
2. Confirm Manufacturer Due Diligence GO.
3. Confirm the CEO-defined permitted action: draft only, approval request or authorized send.
4. Verify recipient, channel and timing scope.
5. Check do-not-contact, prior-contact and authorization records.
6. If authorization is absent, prepare assets only.

## 2. Audit upstream evidence

Read accepted:

- Product Intelligence
- Manufacturer Hunter
- Manufacturer Due Diligence
- Buyer Intelligence
- Pricing Intelligence
- Competition Intelligence
- Relevant CEO decision

Extract only claims suitable for external communication. Build a VERIFIED/ESTIMATED/UNKNOWN register.

## 3. Verify recipient and channels

1. Verify contact name, title, department and company.
2. Verify official email source.
3. Verify LinkedIn professional role.
4. Verify phone and office route.
5. Check domain consistency and impersonation risk.
6. Check last verification date.
7. Use UNKNOWN for any unverified field.
8. Select channels based on evidence, role and relationship stage.

## 4. Diagnose the manufacturer

Answer:

1. What does the company sell and to whom?
2. What is its export maturity?
3. What is its current Turkey status?
4. What entry uncertainty may it face?
5. What commercial motivation is plausible?
6. What risk perception may block it?
7. What proof would make a meeting worthwhile?
8. Why might it reject Atlas?

Separate verified facts from hypotheses.

## 5. Build the manufacturer value proposition

1. State why Türkiye is relevant.
2. State why now, if evidenced.
3. State the risk of traditional distributor or hiring decisions.
4. Define Atlas's low-risk validation role.
5. Define the 90-day pilot outcome.
6. State measurable outputs.
7. Define the low-friction meeting ask.
8. Remove unsupported claims and self-focused language.

## 6. Build the customer sales strategy

1. Select the relevant Turkish buyer segments.
2. Identify the buyer's strongest supported problem.
3. Choose value drivers from price, delivery, maintenance, downtime, equivalence, energy, spares, supply security, compliance or service.
4. Explain why the product can win.
5. Identify technical proof required.
6. Identify what Atlas must not claim.
7. Do not claim named buyers unless accepted Buyer Intelligence supports it.

## 7. Design channel and timing

1. Choose primary and secondary channels.
2. Choose first-contact timing with evidence or label ESTIMATED.
3. Define channel purpose.
4. Set follow-up spacing.
5. Avoid simultaneous channel overload.
6. Respect holidays, time zones and published business hours.
7. Define stop, pause and escalation conditions.

## 8. Generate the complete sequence

Create:

1. Personalized first email
2. LinkedIn connection request
3. LinkedIn first message
4. Follow-up email #1
5. Follow-up email #2
6. Follow-up email #3
7. Phone call script
8. Meeting request
9. Meeting agenda

Each touch must add new value, not merely ask “did you see my email?”

## 9. Build meeting-conversion assets

Create:

- Manufacturer value proposition
- Customer sales strategy
- Risk-reduction message
- ROI/business-impact angle
- Manufacturer-specific positioning
- Meeting agenda
- 90-day pilot proposal

Make the meeting useful even if the manufacturer is not ready to appoint a partner.

## 10. Build objections and FAQ

For every required objection:

1. Diagnose likely meaning.
2. Identify missing evidence.
3. Write a concise response.
4. Reframe around risk reduction.
5. Offer a proportionate next step.
6. Define when to stop.

Build FAQ for the pilot, Atlas role, buyer evidence, confidentiality, costs, exclusivity, reporting and success metrics.

## 11. Build timeline and CRM schedule

For each sequence step record:

- Day/date window
- Channel
- Asset
- Owner
- Objective
- Authorization status
- Completion evidence
- Response branch
- Next action
- Stop condition

No send occurs without explicit authorization for that step.

## 12. Estimate conversion probabilities

1. Use Atlas historical cohort data when sufficient.
2. Otherwise use a cited benchmark and transparent adjustments.
3. Show sample limitations.
4. Give a range, not false precision.
5. Label VERIFIED, ESTIMATED or UNKNOWN.
6. Never invent response or meeting probability.

## 13. Quality and risk review

1. Remove generic claims.
2. Verify every manufacturer-specific fact.
3. Verify every buyer and ROI claim.
4. Remove exclusivity requests from first touch.
5. Remove overpromised sales.
6. Confirm tone is commercial partner, not job applicant.
7. Confirm the ask is specific and low-friction.
8. Confirm privacy, authorization and brand safeguards.

## 14. Apply GO/WATCH/NO-GO

- GO when the exact sequence is truthful, personalized, channel-ready and confidence is sufficient.
- WATCH when a bounded verification is needed.
- NO-GO when contact is inappropriate, unverifiable or brand-damaging.

GO means ready for approval, not sent.

## 15. Apply the stop rule

Stop when GO gates are met and further research is unlikely to materially change the message or channel decision.

Do not delay execution to perfect non-critical details.

## 16. Write and synchronize outputs

1. Write the next `intelligence/outreach/RXXX.md`.
2. Finish with the exact COMMERCIAL ACTION section.
3. Update `database/outreach.csv` with sequence, assets, estimates, authorization and next actions.
4. Update `workspace/active_project.md` with communication status and next gate.
5. Update only Outreach Intelligence-owned dashboard values.
6. Preserve stable IDs, prior history and unrelated content.
7. Cite report ID and verification date.
8. Route GO to Atlas CEO for explicit send authorization.

## Escalation

Escalate sanctions, privacy, impersonation, bribery, deceptive claims, sensitive buyer information, legal terms, brand risk, explicit rejection or any external action outside authorization.

## Completion definition

Work is complete when Atlas has a truthful, personalized, multi-channel sequence and pilot-based meeting proposition that can be approved and executed without inventing facts or improvising the next step.
