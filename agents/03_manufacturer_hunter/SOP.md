# SOP — Manufacturer Hunter

## 1. Verify the Product Intelligence handoff

1. Read the accepted `intelligence/products/RXXX.md`.
2. Confirm status is GO.
3. Extract product boundaries, variants, must-have specifications, certifications, applications, stock model, lead-time constraints and support requirements.
4. Convert those requirements into a manufacturer inclusion/exclusion checklist.
5. If any requirement is decision-critical and unclear, stop and request clarification.

## 2. Prepare the search plan

1. Build keyword families using product names, technical synonyms, standards, applications and manufacturing processes.
2. Build country/language variants for each priority region.
3. Define rejection patterns for distributors, traders, marketplaces and OEM resellers.
4. Set the evidence cutoff and report ID.
5. Search Tier 1 first, then expand deliberately to Tier 2 and Tier 3.
6. Define a stopping rule: stop when enough verified candidates exist to defend Top 10, Top 5 and Top 3.

## 3. Create the lead pool

Use:

- Official company sites
- National industry associations
- Official trade agencies and exporter directories
- Certification bodies and certificate databases
- Relevant trade-fair exhibitor lists
- Standards or approval lists
- Professional company pages
- Credible industry publications

Marketplaces, scraped directories and reseller pages may generate leads but cannot establish manufacturer status.

Capture the source that generated each lead.

## 4. Run the manufacturer test

For each lead:

1. Verify company identity and website.
2. Confirm it makes the in-scope products.
3. Find first-party production-process evidence.
4. Find factory evidence tied to the same entity.
5. Distinguish own-manufactured products from distributed brands.
6. Verify technical documentation and engineering capability.
7. Reject non-manufacturers immediately and record why.

For China, add independent entity, factory and certification corroboration before the company can remain in the evaluated pool.

## 5. Verify company maturity and technical capability

Collect:

- Year founded
- Estimated employee range and source basis
- Factory location and capability
- Relevant products and product families
- Engineering, customization, testing and quality capability
- ISO, CE, API or other relevant certifications
- Technical catalogue and application evidence

A certification logo is not enough. Verify issuer, scope, certificate number or current document when decision-critical.

## 6. Verify export capability

1. Find official export statements, countries, subsidiaries, foreign references or international trade activity.
2. Confirm export documentation, multilingual sales material or international standards.
3. Identify export countries only when explicitly supported.
4. Verify international contact routes.
5. Reject companies with no credible export capability.

Do not infer export capability solely from an English website.

## 7. Investigate Turkey presence

1. Search official distributor/partner locators.
2. Search the manufacturer site for Turkey and Türkiye.
3. Search Turkish-language web sources and company records.
4. Check Turkish distributors for first-party authorization.
5. Check Turkey offices, subsidiaries, employees, projects and trade-fair activity.
6. Classify presence as:
   - NONE FOUND
   - RESELLER ONLY
   - LIMITED / UNCLEAR
   - AUTHORIZED PARTNER
   - STRONG ESTABLISHED PRESENCE
   - UNKNOWN
7. Record the supporting URL and confidence.
8. Reject strong established presence; place unresolved channel evidence on WATCH.

## 8. Verify contacts

Collect separately:

- Export contact
- Export email
- Sales email
- General email
- Phone
- LinkedIn company page

Use only official contact pages or other verifiable first-party sources for emails and phone. Do not infer email patterns. If a named contact lacks a verified title or email, preserve the verified parts and mark the rest `UNKNOWN`.

## 9. Evaluate partnership probability

For each surviving manufacturer:

1. Score Export Readiness.
2. Score Turkey Expansion Potential.
3. Score Communication Quality.
4. Score Technical Strength.
5. Score Commercial Fit.
6. Score Representation Potential.
7. Score Trust.
8. Score raw Risk.
9. Calculate Inverse Risk and Atlas Score.
10. Explain why the company might or might not need Atlas.
11. Identify the strongest objection the manufacturer may have.
12. State what due diligence must verify next.

Apply identical scoring rules across countries.

## 10. Classify and rank

- GO only when every GO threshold and evidence gate is satisfied.
- WATCH when a credible candidate needs bounded verification.
- NO-GO when a rejection rule or structural mismatch applies.

Rank by Atlas Score, then use Trust, Representation Potential, Turkey channel gap and contact readiness as tie-breakers.

Do not rank NO-GO companies in Top 10.

## 11. Build the three commercial tiers

### Top 10 Manufacturers

The ten best due-diligence candidates, with evidence, scores, status, key risk and next verification.

### Top 5 Priority Targets

The five companies with the strongest combination of partner probability, Turkey gap, technical fit and feasible commercial model.

### Top 3 Immediate Outreach Candidates

The three candidates most ready for final due diligence and contact verification. Each must have:

- GO status
- Verified official contact route
- No obvious strong Turkey channel
- Clear Atlas value hypothesis
- Specific first-message angle
- Explicit risk note

This tier does not authorize sending.

## 12. Write and synchronize outputs

1. Write the next `intelligence/manufacturers/RXXX.md`.
2. End with the five required sections in the required order.
3. Update `database/manufacturers.csv` for every evaluated company.
4. Update `workspace/active_project.md` with shortlist and next gate.
5. Update only Manufacturer Hunter-owned values in `dashboard/CEO_DASHBOARD.md`.
6. Preserve stable IDs, source history and unrelated content.
7. Cite the report ID in all updates.
8. Hand GO candidates to Manufacturer Due Diligence.

## Escalation

Escalate sanctions, controlled-goods, bribery, fraud, ownership, safety or legal concerns; ambiguous authorization; conflicting company identity; or any request to contact, negotiate or commit externally.

## Completion definition

Work is complete when Atlas has a small, verified, ranked partner portfolio—not merely a list—and the Top 3 can enter due diligence without basic manufacturer, product, export, Turkey-channel or contact questions being guessed.
